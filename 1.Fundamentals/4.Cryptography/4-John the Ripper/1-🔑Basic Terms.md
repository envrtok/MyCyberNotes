## ğŸ”’ What Makes Hashes Secure?

Hashing functions are designed to be **one-way**:

- Easy to compute a hash from input.
- Extremely hard (computationally infeasible) to reverse a hash back to the original input.

This difficulty ties into the famous **P vs NP problem** in computer science:

- **P (Polynomial Time):** Problems solvable in reasonable time (e.g., sorting a list).
- **NP (Non-deterministic Polynomial Time):** Problems where verifying a solution is easy, but finding it may be very hard.
